🛕 Featured Temples Across India (Readable Format)
1. Amarnath Temple
State: Jammu & Kashmir | City: Near Pahalgam
Deity: Lord Shiva (Ice Lingam)
Historical Significance: Located at an altitude of 16,000 feet, this is a highly revered cave temple where a natural ice lingam forms
. The journey usually starts from Pahalgam, passing through scenic stops like Chandanwari and Sheshnag
.
2. Vaishno Devi
State: Jammu & Kashmir | City: Katra
Deity: Mahakali, Mahalakshmi, Mahasaraswati
Historical Significance: Situated in a dark cave about 46 miles from Jammu, the temple houses the three supreme goddesses in the form of 'Pindis' (sacred stones)
.
3. Badrinath Dham
State: Uttarakhand | City: Badrinath
Deity: Lord Badrinarayan (Vishnu)
Historical Significance: Situated on the banks of the Alaknanda river near the Nar-Narayan mountains, it is one of the supreme pilgrimage sites
. Pilgrims take a holy dip in the nearby Tapt Kund (hot spring) before having darshan
.
4. Kedarnath Temple
State: Uttarakhand | City: Kedarnath
Deity: Lord Shiva (Jyotirlinga)
Historical Significance: One of the twelve Jyotirlingas, this temple is located in the Himalayas near the Mandakini river
. It is believed that Lord Shiva took the form of a bull here, and the temple represents the hump of the bull
.
5. Kashi Vishwanath Temple
State: Uttar Pradesh | City: Varanasi (Kashi)
Deity: Lord Shiva (Jyotirlinga)
Historical Significance: Kashi is considered one of the seven Moksha-puris (cities that grant liberation) and is believed to rest on the trident of Lord Shiva
. The Jyotirlinga here is the center of devotion for millions seeking spiritual salvation
.
6. Naimisharanya (Chakra Tirtha)
State: Uttar Pradesh | City: Naimisharanya (Near Sitapur)
Deity: Chakra Tirtha / Various
Historical Significance: A legendary forest where Lord Brahma's Chakra fell, absorbing its rim (Nemi) into the earth, thus creating the Chakra Tirtha
. It is the sacred site where 88,000 sages performed penance and where the Puranas were narrated
.
7. Shri Ram Janmabhoomi
State: Uttar Pradesh | City: Ayodhya
Deity: Lord Rama
Historical Significance: Ayodhya, situated on the banks of the sacred Sarayu river, is the birthplace of Maryada Purushottam Lord Rama
. Built by Manu, it is hailed as one of the top Moksha-puris
.
8. Hariharnath Temple
State: Bihar | City: Sonepur
Deity: Harihar (Half Vishnu, Half Shiva)
Historical Significance: Located at the confluence of the Ganga and Gandak rivers, it is widely believed to be the site of the legendary battle between Gaj (elephant) and Grah (crocodile), where Lord Vishnu intervened
.
9. Jagannath Temple
State: Odisha | City: Puri
Deity: Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra
Historical Significance: One of the four supreme Dhams, also known as Purushottam Puri or Shankha Kshetra
. The temple is world-famous for its annual Rath Yatra and the Mahaprasad, which is consumed without any rules of untouchability
.
10. Trimbakeshwar
State: Maharashtra | City: Near Nashik
Deity: Lord Shiva (Jyotirlinga)
Historical Significance: This Jyotirlinga temple is located near the Brahmagiri mountain, which is the origin of the sacred Godavari (Gautami) river
. Sage Gautama performed severe penance here to bring the river to earth
.
11. Vitthal Rukmini Temple
State: Maharashtra | City: Pandharpur
Deity: Lord Vitthal (Pandurang) and Rukmini
Historical Significance: Situated on the banks of the Bhima (Chandrabhaga) river, this is the most revered site for the Varkari sect
. Lord Vitthal is said to have stood on a brick here, moved by the devotion of his devotee Pundalik
.
12. Somnath Temple
State: Gujarat | City: Prabhas Patan (Veraval)
Deity: Lord Shiva (Jyotirlinga)
Historical Significance: The very first of the twelve Jyotirlingas, located on the western sea coast
. According to legend, the Moon God (Soma) worshipped Lord Shiva here to cure himself of a curse, hence the name Somnath
.
